Wilderness Flash Events are a members-only Distraction and Diversion released on 17 October 2022 . In this Distraction and Diversion, players work with or against the factions in the Zamorakian Civil War by participating in different short, hourly occurring flash events in various locations around the Wilderness . Some of the events involve only non-combat skilling, some only combat, and some a mix of both. Rewards include experience in the skill involved in the flash event and reward sacks that yield items when opened. Participating in Wilderness Flash Events does yield strange and golden rocks for their respective skills.

Information about the current or upcoming flash event may be obtained from Nikkel and Wandering Ramokee in Edgeville . As with other Distraction and Diversions, Wilderness Flash Events may be favourited through the Minigames interface to make it show in the reset timers of the game clock interface, and added to the Activity Tracker . Choosing the "teleport to lodestone" option teleports the player to the Edgeville lodestone .

Players can check how many events they have completed using Quick Chat , which is useful to those seeking to complete the Walk on the Wildy Side achievements. This tracking is capped at 2,000 events. Also, it may be of note to know that each part of the event counts as an event completed, for example; the Evil Bloodwood Tree event counts as three events not just one.

Overview [ edit | edit source ]

Each flash event is broadcast five minutes before it starts, at :55 each hour, and at the moment the event begins, at :00 each hour. Nikkel and Wandering Ramokee in Edgeville may be spoken to beforehand to learn where the next event will happen. Completing the achievement Far-Reaching and Wild-Ranging will allow the player to immediately arrive at the event location through a right-click "teleport" option on either NPC or by operating a Wilderness sword 4 anywhere. The teleport has a 45-minute cooldown.

The events occur in a fixed sequence. As there are 14 different hourly events, this means that they stagger throughout the day in the players' local times. While the event start time is synchronised at the start for all worlds, completion or failure of the event can vary.

However, there is a 15-minute timer in which the player may not receive rewards from additional events after switching worlds. This includes each stage of multi-reward events. For example, if you get the rewards for the first stage of Evil Bloodwood Tree, fail the second stage, and then hop worlds and complete the second stage in another world, you will not get any rewards for the second or third stage.

The message given for failing to receive a reward due to the timer is You are ineligible for rewards from this event, as you have claimed rewards from a flash event on world [number] within the last 15 minutes. Return to this world to continue earning rewards.

The events' locations are fixed; each event always happens in the same location. The following map shows the location of all the events.

Each event has two targets: one personal target, and one collective event target. Both must be met within a five-minute time limit for participants to receive the full rewards. Achieving only the personal target yields a reduced experience reward while failing to achieve the personal target results in no rewards even if the collective target is reached. The personal target for non-combat events (with the exception of Surprising Seedlings) scales inversely with the number of players in the area (down to a minimum); the more players, the less each of them needs to contribute. The collective event target scales directly; the more players, the more they need to contribute as a whole.

In skilling events, once the timer expires, the group has an additional 30 seconds to hand in any remaining resources to ensure that other players can hand in and meet the collective goal. This 30-second timer will automatically start if the collective goal is met before the timer expires.

Players will obtain a sack of wild rewards when any event is successfully completed. For the special events, they additionally receive a sack of very wild rewards . If the event fails, then the player gets a reduced experience reward if they met their personal goal; otherwise, nothing is obtained at all.

Dying and logging out during an event both reset the player's personal progress, but leaving the area otherwise does not (so players under attack can teleport out and return when it is safe). As long as the player's progress is not reset they will receive their reward as long as the event is completed. The player must also remain on the same world if the event requires them to hand in resources.

Strategy [ edit | edit source ]

In order to receive rewards, it is important to fully participate in each event. Players who successfully reach their individual contribution requirement will receive a sack of wild rewards upon event completion. Before participating in combat events, the player should make sure that they have at least two free spaces in their inventory, or one in inventory and one in bank. Participating in skilling events, the player should reserve enough spaces in their inventory for gathering resources, minimum two spaces.

If both the player's bank and inventory are full, the rewards will be temporarily stored with Nikkel and the wandering ramokee at Edgeville. If the player hops worlds or logs out before claiming the rewards, they will be lost and unrecoverable.

General strategy [ edit | edit source ]

  • Preparing: five minutes before the upcoming event starts, the relevant event and location hint will be broadcasted as a chat message. The player should arrive at the destination on the dot because of the competitive nature. The player may talk to Nikkel or the Wandering Ramokee to get information about the next event and its location after the current event starts.
    • Players who have completed the Far-Reaching and Wild-Ranging achievement can interact with Nikkel or the Wandering Ramokee to teleport to the active event every 45 minutes. The teleportation option is offered by talking to either and asking about the next event five minutes before the event starts, or after the event has started by right-clicking them or asking them about the current event.
    • Players who do not have the teleport unlocked may find it useful to complete the hard Wilderness achievements as this allows quick teleports to the Wilderness lodestone and allows the destination to be selected when using obelisks, or a portable obelisk , as many of the event locations are near them.
    • It is a good idea to opt out of PvP prior to starting an event as player killers can and will appear at events.
  • General skilling: gather resources and hand them in to the nearby NPC before the overall quota is reached. After either the quota is reached or five minutes elapse, all skilling plots will disappear, and players have an additional 30 seconds to hand in any remaining resources to the NPC nearby before the event ends. Inexperienced players should use highly populated worlds. Low population worlds are more competitive as the event goal tends to be reached quickly and the personal goal is higher, so they might not be able to reach it in time.
  • General combat: the player has five minutes to deal at least 70,000 damage and kill up to 100 enemies. After the final enemy dies, if the player has not dealt the required damage, the event will not be considered completed for the player. If five minutes elapse before all of the enemies are killed, all participants will fail the event. This should be unlikely in most cases, however.
    • Prioritise using AoE or multi-target abilities whenever possible, such as Greater Chain into Omnipower , or Threads of Fate with Soul Sap and Volley of Souls . This is very useful in hectic events or when the player arrives as the event is nearing its end.
      • When using targeted Magma Tempest , it can be used on the minimap rather than on the game screen. This avoids having to click on the screen itself, as an accidental click on another player would prompt PvP messages and not use the ability.
    • Having target cycle keybound is extremely useful, especially when events are crowded with other players, making it hard to see enemies.
    • It is recommended (especially for low level players) to use a busy world for combat events, as they are less likely to be targeted by mobs; in low-population events, mobs may have too little health and kills are racked up too quickly for inexperienced players to get their damage goal before the event ends. Tier 70+ equipment are recommended before attempting to do combat events on low-population worlds.
  • Multi-stage events: in the early stages, make sure that the encounter reaches the overall target so that the event will continue to the final stage, even if that means missing out on normal sacks. This does not affect the player's eligibility to get the special sacks.
  • Try to avoid dying during an event as this will cause the player to lose their personal progress.
  • If the player fails an event due to not reaching the personal target before the collective target is reached, then it is possible to switch to another world and try again as long as the event has not ended on that world. However, personal progress will be reset.

In Spider Swarm, up to a hundred spiders spawn continuously. The players have five minutes to kill the amount required, with the player having to deal at least 70,000 damage for the event to be considered successful for them. The number of spiders required to kill scales with the number of players participating in the event, determined by the expression min ⁡ ( 1 0 0 , 2 0 + 2 N ) where N is the number of players involved. The spiders that may spawn are dungeon spiders , ice spiders , and night spiders . Successful completion of the event yields a combat XP lamp and a sack of wild rewards .

The event's description in the Minigames interface is "Kill the swarm of spiders."

Strategy [ edit | edit source ]

The NopeNopeNope player-owned farm perk works. Players may become poisoned; the anti-poison totem , poison purge aura and Venomblood perk all work in negating damage. Bring combat gear and food, as 100 spiders can deal a lot of damage if not enough players are present. The spiders exclusively use melee, so Protect from Melee or Deflect Melee is useful especially to lower-level players.

In Stryke the Wyrm, a Wildywyrm will spawn north of the Wilderness pond reachable with a waka canoe . It is a short distance south of Wilderness sword 's teleport to the herb patch .

Players must kill the Wildywyrm in under 5 minutes for the event to be considered successful, with players having to deal at least 35,000 damage personally to gain rewards. The Wildywyrm's life points scale with the number of players participating in the event, determined by the expression m i n ( 4 0 0 , 2 + 3 N ) * 5 0 0 0 0 where N is the number of players involved. The Wildywyrm despawns 5 minutes after the start of the event if it isn't killed, causing the event to fail. Killing the Wildywyrm in time with enough damage dealt yields Slayer experience scaling to Slayer level, a sack of wild rewards , a sack of very wild rewards , a wyrm reward gland and, only to the player that did the most damage, one pile of searing ashes .

The event's description in the Minigames interface is "Destroy the Wildywyrm!"

Strategy [ edit | edit source ]

The Wildywyrm is essentially a larger version of the lava strykewyrm - capable of using all three combat styles against players. It will only melee if its target is next to them. As its ranged attack, it launches numerous projectiles around it, which will proceed to hit all players in a five-tile radius from where it landed. The magic attack will target one player, but can bounce to others nearby.

Periodically, the wyrm will burrow into the ground, dragging all players in an 8-tile radius surrounding it to itself, before bursting from the ground. During this attack, the wyrm will be immune to damage and also clear out any bleeds inflicted on it. If the player is caught in the attack, they will take 50% of their current health as damage. Using Anticipation or Freedom prior to the burrow will prevent the drag, or alternatively players may run away before the wyrm unburrows to avoid the damage.

Unlike its lesser counterparts and previous incarnations, the wyrm does not have a stomp attack, thus players can avoid all damage (and the burrow mechanic) by having the main target stand underneath it. The wyrm will quickly choose another target if this occurs however, so the primary tank should step out after every three attacks then walk under the wyrm to retain aggression. The tank can still attack underneath the wyrm via hotkeys and clicking underneath it.

In Butterfly Swarm, a swarm of butterflies spawns near the obelisk south-east of the Wilderness Crater lodestone .

Player must catch the magical butterflies and avoid the poisonous ones ; if you catch the poisonous ones (they're a different colour) they'll sting you, dealing one big hit of poison damage. Players have a maximum of five minutes to catch the butterflies and give them to the demon hunter in the area. Each player must give at least max ⁡ ( 1 0 , 2 0 − N ) butterflies while the players collectively must hand in 1 0 + 2 2 N butterflies, where N is the number of players involved. These numbers scale depending on the number of players participating in the event. The butterflies leave either after five minutes or when the collective event progress has been met, whichever happens first. After the butterflies have left, players still have 30 seconds to hand any remaining ones in before the event ends. If both the player's personal and collective event butterflies handed in goals were met when the event ends, the event is considered successful and they are given Hunter experience depending on their Hunter level and a sack of wild rewards .

The event's description in the Minigames interface is "Gather butterflies from the swarm".

Strategy [ edit | edit source ]

It is recommended to have lots of backpack space before starting this event to reduce the frequency of handing in resources. This is one of the harder skilling events in terms of meeting the requirement and may have a significant chance of failing or succeeding narrowly if there are too many leechers. Players who want to avoid taking poison damage should only catch the colourful, non-dangerous butterflies. The anti-poison totem , poison purge aura and Venomblood perk all work in negating damage from the poisonous butterflies.

In the special event King Black Dragon Rampage, players must kill the King Black Dragon . The event occurs south-west of the Daemonheim castle, north of the waterfiends .

The King Black Dragon spawns with a freezing black dragon , a poison black dragon , and a shocking black dragon , and is immune to damage while any of these are alive. Furthermore, the King Black Dragon can summon spires in the area which protect all the dragons from damage. The spires must first be destroyed by attacking them, and then the minions killed before finally killing the King Black Dragon. All the dragons in the area are capable of dealing significant damage. The King Black Dragon cannot be killed with a deathtouched dart .

Each player participating in the event must deal at least 35,000 damage to the King Black Dragon for the event to be considered successful, depending on the number of players participating in the event. Damage dealt to the King Black Dragon's minions and the spires does not count towards the personal target. The event ends in failure after five minutes have passed, or if the King Black Dragon dies before the player has dealt the required amount of damage. Succeeding in the event yields a combat XP lamp , a sack of wild rewards , and a sack of very wild rewards , and can also yield the king black dragon scale .

The event's description in the Minigames interface is "Stop the King Black Dragon!"

Strategy [ edit | edit source ]

This encounter is much more dangerous than regular combat events, so the player should adequately prepare as the chance of dying is high. The King Black Dragon will be immune to damage while there are spires and/or minions active, so the player should attack those first. When attacking the King Black Dragon or his minions, dragon slayer perk and ability both work. Avoid flame vortice areas as these will quickly kill the player if not avoided. In events with many people, the vortices will be invisible due to rendering issues and thus players should watch their health.

The King Black Dragon mostly uses magic attacks that hit numerous players at once, using Protect/Deflect Magic reduces this damage. If one of the lesser dragons is on the player, the prayer can also reduce the damage of their dragonfire attacks, which would otherwise hit very hard (3000+).

Players can also instantly kill or severely weaken the lesser dragons by performing Detonate on the area they would spawn in. The dragons will initially spawn with solo scale stats, and sufficiently damaging Detonates will instantly kill them even though their health would scale upwards. If Detonate is incorrectly set off, the dragons will survive, although with significantly less health.

In Surprising Seedlings, four seedlings spawn on the ground around the pond. Players must nurture them to make them grow into strange flower sprouts , and finally into poisonous hollyhock , Wilderness iris , and Wilderness rose bushes. Players must then harvest the iris and rose bushes to obtain Wilderness irises and Wilderness roses , while attempting to harvest the poisonous hollyhock causes one heavy hit of poison damage to the player, capable of dealing between 1,145 and 2,189 damage. The poisonous hollyhock can be dug out to make space for new seedlings to nurture to avoid the damage. The flowers must be given to the Zamorakian farmer in the area. Each player needs to give at least seven flowers for the event to be considered a success for them. The total amount required scales linearly with the number of players participating in the event, determined by the expression 5 + 1 0 N where N is the number of players. The flower bushes disappear after five minutes or once the collective requirement has been met, whichever comes first, after which the players still have 30 seconds to hand in their remaining flowers. Successful completion of the event yields Farming experience depending on the player's Farming level and a sack of wild rewards .

This event's description in the Minigames interface is "Gather flowers from the plants".

Strategy [ edit | edit source ]

There are four seedlings surrounding the pond. Nurturing any seedling will allow it to soon grow into Wilderness irises , roses , or Poisonous hollyhocks . There can only be a maximum of two poisonous hollyhocks. If the player leaves the two poisonous hollyhocks alone, the remaining seedlings will always be either irises or roses. It is recommended to force the two poisonous hollyhocks to grow at the northern spawns because the southern spawns are much closer together, thus reducing time spent walking between spawns. This strategy allows for the event to be completed much faster than nurturing all four seedlings and digging up poisonous hollyhocks. Unlike most events, this can easily be completed quickly by solo players so there is little advantage in going to a busy world. Similarly to the butterflies event, the anti-poison totem, poison purge aura and Venomblood perk all work in negating damage from the hollyhocks.

Infernal Star is a special event consisting of two sub-events: Infernal Star: Mine and Infernal Star: Fight. The event occurs just north-east of the Chaos Temple .

In the Infernal Star: Mine event players mine the infernal star . They have five minutes to gain enough progress, with each participant requiring at least max ⁡ ( 2 5 , 4 5 − N ) personal mining progress and the event collectively requiring 1 5 + 5 5 N event mining progress for the event to be considered successful, where N is the number of players involved. The collective event requirement scales with the number of players participating in the event. Each swing yields one progress. The players can take damage from randomly determined swings announced with You feel heat building inside the star! Damage can be completely avoided by moving at least two game squares from your current one or significantly reduced with the protect from magic prayer. If personal and collective progress goals are not both met by the end of the event time, the star will despawn, and the linked next event will not start. The event ends once the time runs out or the collective target is reached, whichever comes first. For a successful completion, a sack of wild rewards and Mining experience scaled to the player's Mining level is given. This event's description in the Minigames interface is "Mine the fallen star to its centre."

In the Infernal Star: Fight event the players fight the pyrefiends that continuously spawn in the area. The event ends in five minutes or when the target number of pyrefiends have been killed, whichever comes first. The number of kills required scales with the number of players participating in the event, determined by the expression min ⁡ ( 1 0 0 , 2 0 + 2 N ) , where N is the number of players involved. Players must personally deal 70,000 damage for this event to be considered successful. Successful completion of the event yields a combat XP lamp , a sack of wild rewards , and a sack of very wild rewards . This event's description in the Minigames interface is "Kill all the pyrefiends."

Strategy [ edit | edit source ]

This event has two stages, each of which will count as an event completion and give a sack of wild rewards. In the first stage the star causes damage to a 3x3 area centred on the player, so moving to mine the star outside of this area avoids the damage in a quicker manner than moving away from the star and then back in. The second stage involves combat, so the player should bring their best combat equipment. AoE abilities will help in increasing damage. Players can stand north of the star after the event ends to minimise the chances of a pyrefiend attacking them.

It is recommended to do this on a busy world (especially for lower-level players without at least tier 80 gear) as it greatly increases the time available to deal the required 70k damage and reduces the chance of being attacked by pyrefiends.

In Lost Souls, the reanimated skeleton appears in Forgotten Cemetery who must be given ectoplasm to succeed in the event. The ectoplasm can be collected in two ways:

  • Laying ghosts to rest, yielding 10 Prayer experience and one ectoplasm each.
  • Killing Graveside Phantoms , yielding regular combat experience and three ectoplasm each when killed.

Both ghosts and Graveside Phantoms spawn continuously for five minutes or until the collective event target has been met, whichever happens first. To succeed in the event, the player must personally hand in at least max ⁡ ( 1 5 , 3 0 − N ) ectoplasm, while the collective event target scales linearly with the number of players participating in the event, determined by the expression 5 + 4 0 N where N is the number of players. Both requirements depend on the number of players participating in the event. After the ghosts and Graveside Phantoms have left, players still have 30 seconds to contribute any remaining ectoplasm.

After the event ends and if both personal and collective targets were met, the player receives Prayer experience depending on their Prayer level and a sack of wild rewards .

This event's description in the Minigames interface is "Gather ectoplasm from the wandering ghosts."

Strategy [ edit | edit source ]

Have lots of backpack space before starting this event to reduce the frequency of gathering and handing in resources. You need to damage graveside phantoms to gain ectoplasm from one when it is killed. The phantoms are aggressive, so players should bring some combat gear to fight them off. They will shortly respawn after being subdued.

Ghosts provide one ectoplasm, while phantoms provide three at a time. If the player's inventory is full, any excess ectoplasm is destroyed. During downtime when no phantoms are present, harvest from ghosts.

In Ramokee Incursion, a combination of up to 100 ramokee bloodragers , deathslingers , and stormbringers spawn continuously. The number of enemies that spawn scales with the number of players in the area, determined by the expression min ⁡ ( 1 0 0 , 2 0 + 2 N ) where N is the number of players involved. The players have five minutes to kill them all, with the player having to deal 70,000 damage for the event to be considered successful for them. The event ends once the time runs out or all the enemies have been killed, whichever happens first. Successful completion of the event yields a combat XP lamp and a sack of wild rewards .

The event's description in the Minigames interface is "Destroy the Ramokee Incursion."

Bring combat gear and food, as the ramokees can deal a lot of damage if not enough players are present.

In Displaced Energy, displaced energy is harvested from lost wisps . Players are required to give at least max ⁡ ( 2 0 , 3 0 − N ) energy to the Zamorakian diviner to be eligible for rewards, and a total of 1 0 + 3 2 N energy must be given collectively for the event to be considered successful, where N is the number of players involved in the area. Occasionally the lost wisps yield unstable energy that causes an explosion dealing heavy damage up to 2,172 to the player if they move from their current game square with it in their backpack . If this happens, the player will get a message saying they are stunned for six seconds, and the stun debuff icon will appear, but the player will not actually be stunned. Players can click the "Stabilise" option on the unstable energy to transform it into displaced energy safely. The wisps disappear when five minutes have passed or when the collective hand-in target has been reached, whichever comes first. After this the players still have 30 seconds to hand in their remaining energy before the event ends. Successful completion of the event yields Divination experience depending on the player's Divination level and a sack of wild rewards .

This event's description in the Minigames interface is "Gather energy from the lost wisps."

Strategy [ edit | edit source ]

It is recommended to have at least twenty inventory spaces before starting the event. Some food is recommended for this event, as the hazard is very hard to react to and deals more damage than others.

Teleporting using Trollheim teleport and running east is the quickest way to access this event until the completion of Far-Reaching and Wild-Ranging .

Evil Bloodwood Tree is a special event consisting of three sub-events: Evil Bloodwood Tree: Feed, Evil Bloodwood Tree: Chop, and Evil Bloodwood Tree: Burn. The event occurs just south of the grave and a statue on the green patch of land south-west of the Wilderness Crater lodestone .

Evil Bloodwood Tree: Feed yields Farming experience . Its description in the Minigames interface is "Feed the sapling ground bones." During this phase the players must first take bones from the four skeletons surrounding the bloody sapling . The bones must then be ground before the sapling is nurtured with them. A total max ⁡ ( 3 5 , 1 1 0 − N ) bones must be contributed personally to succeed in the event, while the shared event progress required is 5 0 + 1 7 0 N , where N is the number of players involved. Both requirements scale according to the number of players participating in the event. After five minutes or when the collective target has been reached, whichever happens first, the skeletons disappear. Players then have 30 seconds to nurture the sapling with the remaining bones, and the tree grows into an evil bloodwood tree , proceeding the event to the next phase and giving the players who contributed enough Farming experience depending on their Farming level and a sack of wild rewards , or ends if the players were unsuccessful.

Evil Bloodwood Tree: Chop yields Woodcutting experience . Its description in the Minigames interface is "Chop down the evil bloodwood tree." During this phase the players must chop down the evil bloodwood tree , damaging it and yielding evil tree kindling to be used in the next phase. Chopping must be done max ⁡ ( 4 0 , 8 0 − N ) times personally to succeed in the event, while 2 5 + 1 0 0 N shared event progress is required, scaling according to the number of players participating in the event, where N is the number of players involved. The player can continue to chop the tree after their inventory is full, but no more kindling will be received. After five minutes the event ends or when the collective target has been reached the event ends, and the tree changes into a butchered bloodwood tree , proceeding the event to the next phase and giving the players who contributed enough Woodcutting experience depending on their Woodcutting level and a sack of wild rewards , or ends if the players were unsuccessful.

Evil Bloodwood Tree: Burn yields Firemaking experience . Its description in the Minigames interface is "Burn the remains of the evil bloodwood tree." Players must damage the butchered bloodwood tree by burning evil tree kindling collected in previous phase and harvested from the surrounding fire spirits to succeed in the event. In total max ⁡ ( 4 0 , 4 5 − N ) kindling must be burnt personally to succeed in the event, while 5 0 + 5 5 N kindling burnt by all the players participating collectively in the event is required, where N is the number of players involved. The collective event progress scales according to the number of players participating in the event. After five minutes or when the collective target has been reached, whichever happens first, the fire spirits disappear. The players then have 30 seconds to damage the tree, and if the players contributed enough both personally and collectively, they are given Firemaking experience depending on their Firemaking level, a sack of wild rewards , and a sack of very wild rewards .

In all of the phases above, evil roots lash at the players, binding and stunning them momentarily while also dealing a small amount of damage if not avoided.

Strategy [ edit | edit source ]

No items are necessary for this event, so simply arrive with an empty inventory to begin the event.

As this is a three-stage event, the player will have one slot occupied by a sack of wild rewards after completion of the first event. Do not open the sack and simply chop on the tree until the second stage ends, to immediately dump 27 kindling when the third and final stage begins.

After that, simply harvest the wisps until the event is complete.

Rewards [ edit | edit source ]

Each event yields an experience reward and a sack of wild rewards upon successful completion. The Infernal Star and Evil Tree events are comprised of two stages and three stages respectively, and yield a sack of wild rewards on completion of each stage.

The special events (Stryke the Wyrm, King Black Dragon Rampage, Infernal Star, and Evil Bloodwood Tree) also yield a sack of very wild rewards upon successful completion of the final stage. This sack has different rewards, such as dragon rider boots and gloves , the draconic visage , and black dragon eggs . Completing the King Black Dragon Rampage event additionally gives a chance at Last Riders and a King black dragon scale , and also awards one kill count against the King Black Dragon boss and ten Marks of War . The chance for Last Riders is significantly improved from the normal King Black Dragon fight.

The WildyWyrm event yields wyrm reward gland , which has a chance of a wyrm heart , wyrm scalp , wyrm spike , though fury sharks are much more common.

Any unique items obtained from the events will count towards their respective collection logs.

Staying in the event area is not required to obtain the rewards. Staying logged into the world where the event was completed in is, though.

Unique rewards [ edit | edit source ]

Obelisk shards are tradeable, single-use teleports to Wilderness obelisks . The portable obelisk is a non-tradable, infinite-use version of the obelisk shard. Shards can only be obtained from normal sacks, while the obelisk can be obtained from both. If the player has completed the hard Wilderness achievements , they will be able to select which obelisk to teleport to. Both of the items only work up to level 30 Wilderness if the player is opted into PvP.

A dark onyx core may very rarely be received from special events. It can be used to create three enchantments , one for each alchemical onyx jewellery. The core itself is not tradable, but the enchantments are.
